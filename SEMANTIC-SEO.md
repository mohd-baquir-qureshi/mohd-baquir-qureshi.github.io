## Why Semantic SEO Matters

Traditional keyword-based SEO is no longer sufficient. Modern search engines use semantic understanding (NLP, knowledge graphs, entity recognition) to rank content, and AI-powered search engines (Perplexity, Google SGE, Bing Copilot) directly consume structured content to generate answers. To rank well in both traditional and AI search, your content needs:

1. **Structured Data (Schema.org/JSON-LD)**: Machine-readable markup that search engines and AI models can parse
2. **Semantic HTML**: Proper heading hierarchy, semantic elements (`<article>`, `<section>`, `<nav>`, `<aside>`)
3. **Topical Authority**: Deep, interconnected content clusters that establish expertise
4. **Entity Optimization**: Clear entity definitions (Person, Organization, Role) for knowledge graph inclusion
5. **LLM-Ready Content**: Dedicated files (`llms.txt`, `AGENTS.md`) that AI models can directly consume
